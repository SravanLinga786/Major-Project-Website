7 things which prevent, control and mitigate chili leaf diseases for farmers.

Prevention

#1

Well-Drained Soil

Ensure proper soil drainage to prevent waterlogged conditions, which can contribute to the development of certain diseases.

#2

#2

Crop Rotation

Avoid planting chili peppers in the same location every season to reduce the buildup of pathogens in the soil.

#3

Sanitation

Keep the farming area clean by removing and properly disposing of crop debris, weeds, and any infected plant material.
Thoroughly clean farming equipment, tools, and containers to prevent the spread of diseases.

#3

#4

Proper Spacing

Plant chili peppers with adequate spacing to ensure good air circulation, reducing humidity around the plants and minimizing disease spread.

#5

#5

Proper Watering

Water the plants at the base rather than overhead to minimize moisture on the leaves, which can create a favorable environment for diseases.
Use drip irrigation systems to provide consistent moisture without wetting the foliage excessively.
